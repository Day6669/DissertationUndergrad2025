Deal between Trump and Mexican leader for one-month pause in tariffs

Last night the leaders agreed to pause US tariffs for a month to allow fresh negotiations. Mexico has agreed to send 10,000 members of its national guard "to prevent drug trafficking from Mexico to the US, in particular of fentany!", Sheinbaum said. In return, the US had agreed to work to prevent high-powered weapons crossing the border into Mexico. Trump confirmed the deal shortly afterwards on his Truth Social network. He said 10,000 Mexican soldiers would be "specifically designated to stop the flow of fentanyl, and illegal migrants into our country": Negotiations involving senior Mexican officials, the US secretary of state, Marco Rubio, the US treasury secretary, Scott Bessent, and the US commerce secretary, Howard Lutnick would take place during the pause, he said.

Markets sold off sharply worldwide in what was described as a "Trump tariff tantrum" before recovering some of their losses after news broke of the deal with Mexico. Wall Street fell in early trading, with the S&P 500 falling by almost 2%, while share prices in Europe followed a sharp move lower in Asia.

In London, the FTSE 100 dropped 1.4% from Friday's record high, before clawing back some of its losses to trade down 1%.

Trump indicated on Sunday that the EU would be next to face tariffs but did not say when. "They don't take our cars, they don't take our farm products. They take almost nothing and we take everything from them," he told reporters.

EU leaders meeting at an informal summit in Brussels yesterday said Europe would be prepared to fight back if the US imposed tariffs, but also called for negotiation. The French president, Emmanuel Macron, said if the EU were attacked in its commercial interests it would have to "make itself respected and thus react".

The German chancellor, Olaf Scholz, said the bloc could respond with its own tariffs, but stressed it was better to find agreement on trade.
